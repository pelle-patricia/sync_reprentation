AbstractÂ¶

Phase Locked Loops are nonlinear devices that sense the phase of a sinusoidal signal and generate internally another sinusoidal signal that closely reproduces that phase. Making use of a feedback closed loop, an internal sinusoidal oscillator of variable frequency is driven to be in lock with the instantaneous phase of the input signal, following its fluctuation very precisely. PLLs are widely used in communications systems, including FM demodulation, frequency multiplexing, and frequency synthesizers, particularly due to its ability to automatically track periodic signals even under very severe noise conditions. Besides these classical applications, the similarity between biological evidence displaying active phenomena of synchronous behavior in our auditory system and the PLL operation, motivates us to test this device in the area of speech processing. In this chapter, along with the description of the normal operation of a PLLs and its design, some applications that resemble that biological behavior are described. Specifically, it can be shown that the combination between a passive filter and a PLL has a close relation with the characteristic frequency described for the auditory nerve fibers under sinusoidal signals stimulation. Finally, a system which is able to detect pitch variations of harmonic complex sounds is presented. It uses many of those tandem passive filter-PLL and combines their outputs in a final statistical harmonic consensus detector stage. Both PLL robustness to noise as well as redundancy present in the consensus stage results in a pitch detector system with very low error rate.

Keywords: Auditory Experiments, Synchronism, Pitch.
